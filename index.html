About Brian

Data that transforms the business — and the culture

I've spent two decades at the intersection of financial services and enterprise data — from the quantitative foundations of mortgage credit risk to building modern data platforms at scale across brokerage, insurance, and fintech.

Data initiatives fail not because the technology is wrong, but because the culture isn't ready. I've made it my career's work to build organisations where data isn't a back-office function — it's a shared language owned by the whole business.

Most recently as VP of Enterprise Data at The Zebra, and before that as SVP at LPL Financial, I've led initiatives spanning data engineering modernisation, self-serve analytics, governance frameworks, and GenAI adoption.

Leadership Philosophy

How I lead

01
Culture before technology

The most sophisticated data platform fails if the organisation doesn't trust data. Data transformation is a people problem first.

02
Lead from behind

Creating conditions for a team to excel — giving them clarity, removing blockers, trusting their expertise — consistently beats command-and-control.

03
Business outcomes, always

Data work is only as valuable as its impact on the business. I hold every initiative to a measurable standard — revenue, risk, efficiency, or CX.

04
Speak the language of the business

FS organisations have specific languages — compliance, risk, AUM, policy lifecycle. 20 years of fluency earns a seat at the real table.

05
Embrace AI without losing judgment

GenAI is transformative for FS — for CX, compliance, knowledge management. But deploying responsibly requires deep domain knowledge and rigorous data foundations.

06
Invest in people obsessively

The best data platforms I've built were built by exceptional people I found, developed, and retained. Talent strategy is the highest-leverage thing a data leader does.
