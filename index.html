About Brian

Brian Cronin
VP of Enterprise Data
Financial Services & Insurtech · Charlotte, NC

I've spent two decades at the intersection of financial services and enterprise data — from the quantitative foundations of mortgage credit risk to building modern data platforms at scale across brokerage, insurance, and fintech.

Data initiatives fail not because the technology is wrong, but because the culture isn't ready. I've made it my career's work to build data-centric organisations where data isn't a back-office function — it's a shared language, a strategic asset, and a driver of measurable business outcomes.

Across senior VP and VP-level roles in financial services, I've led cross-functional initiatives spanning data engineering modernisation, self-serve analytics, data governance frameworks, GenAI adoption, and change management — always connecting data work to revenue, risk, and customer experience.

What I Bring

The most effective data leaders combine strategic vision, technical credibility, and the ability to drive cultural change at scale. Over 20+ years I've developed exactly that combination across the full spectrum of financial services.

Leadership Philosophy

How I lead

01
Culture before technology

The most sophisticated data platform fails if the organisation doesn't trust or know how to use data. I invest equally in data literacy, confidence, and cultural change as I do in infrastructure. Data transformation is a people problem first.

02
Lead from behind

Creating conditions for a team to excel — giving them clarity, removing blockers, trusting their expertise — consistently beats command-and-control. I've written about this publicly and lived it across every role.

03
Business outcomes, always

Data work is only as valuable as its impact on the business. I hold every initiative to a measurable standard — revenue, risk reduction, operational efficiency, or customer experience.

04
Speak the language of the business

Financial services organisations have specific languages — compliance, risk, AUM, advisor experience, policy lifecycle. Twenty years of that fluency is what earns a data leader a seat at the real table.

05
Embrace AI without losing judgment

Generative AI and LLMs are genuinely transformative for FS — for CX, compliance, and knowledge management. But responsible deployment requires deep domain knowledge, rigorous data foundations, and strong governance.

06
Invest in people obsessively

The best data platforms I've built were built by exceptional people I found, developed, and retained. Talent strategy, mentorship, and creating an environment where data professionals can do their best work is the highest-leverage thing a data leader does.
